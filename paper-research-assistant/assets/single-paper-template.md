## TL;DR

3-5 sentences explaining the core contribution and why it matters.

## Problem

What problem the paper tries to solve, and what prior gap or limitation motivates it.

## Method

Core method, key assumptions, technical route, and what is actually new or reused.

## Evidence

Main experiments, datasets, metrics, baselines, controls, figures, tables, or theoretical evidence.

## Claims vs Evidence

Which claims are strongly supported, partially supported, weakly supported, or not established from the available text.

## Strengths

What is genuinely valuable: method, framing, evidence, dataset, benchmark, analysis, or practical insight.

## Weaknesses

Limits in method, experiment design, scope, assumptions, reporting, reproducibility, or writing.

## Relation To My Research

How this paper relates to the user's stated research direction. Include only when the user provides that context; otherwise say what context would be needed.

## Follow-up Ideas

Concrete reproduction, extension, comparison, ablation, benchmark, or theoretical next step.

## Confidence

Separate `From paper`, `Inference`, `Suggestion`, and `Unknown`.
